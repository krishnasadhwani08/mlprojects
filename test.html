Chapter 1

Chance Events

Randomness is all around us. Probability theory is the mathematical framework that allows us to analyze chance events in a logically sound manner. The probability of an event is a number indicating how likely that event will occur.

A classic example is a fair coin toss. The probability of flipping heads or tails is 1/2.

Chapter 2

Expectation

The expectation of a random variable is a number that attempts to capture the center of that random variable's distribution. It can be interpreted as the long-run average of many independent samples.

E[X] = Σ x · P(x)

For a fair die: (1+2+3+4+5+6)/6 = 3.5

Chapter 3

Variance

Whereas expectation provides a measure of centrality, the variance of a random variable quantifies the spread of that random variable's distribution.

Var(X) = E[(X - E[X])²]
